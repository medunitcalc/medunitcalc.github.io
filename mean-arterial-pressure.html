Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP)

Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP) is the average arterial pressure during a single cardiac cycle and is used to assess organ perfusion.

MAP Formula

MAP = (Systolic BP + 2 × Diastolic BP) ÷ 3

Example

Systolic BP: 120 mmHg
Diastolic BP: 80 mmHg

MAP = 93 mmHg

Normal Values

  • Normal: 70 – 100 mmHg
  • < 65 mmHg: risk of poor perfusion
  • > 100 mmHg: increased vascular resistance

Clinical Importance

MAP is widely used in medical education to evaluate circulatory adequacy and guide hemodynamic assessment.

Limitations

MAP should always be interpreted within the clinical context and not used alone for diagnosis.
