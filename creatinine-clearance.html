Creatinine Clearance (CrCl)

Creatinine Clearance (CrCl) measures kidney function by assessing the clearance of creatinine from the blood.

Formula (Cockcroft-Gault)

CrCl (mL/min) = [(140 - age) × weight (kg)] ÷ [72 × serum creatinine (mg/dL)]
Multiply by 0.85 if female

Interpretation

CrCl (mL/min)Kidney Function
> 90Normal
60 – 89Mild decrease
30 – 59Moderate decrease
15 – 29Severe decrease
< 15Kidney failure / dialysis

Example

Male patient, 60 years old
Weight = 70 kg
Serum Creatinine = 1.2 mg/dL

Calculation:
CrCl = [(140 - 60) × 70] ÷ (72 × 1.2) ≈ 64.8 mL/min
Interpretation: Mild decrease in kidney function

Clinical Notes

  • Consider age, weight, and gender when interpreting CrCl.
  • Less accurate in extreme body weights or unstable renal function.
  • Used for drug dosing adjustments, especially nephrotoxic drugs.
