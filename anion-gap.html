Anion Gap (AG)

The Anion Gap (AG) is calculated to help identify the cause of metabolic acidosis by measuring the difference between main cations and anions in the blood.

Formula

AG = Na⁺ - (Cl⁻ + HCO₃⁻)

Normal Range

AG (without K⁺ correction): 8 – 12 mmol/L

Interpretation

Anion GapInterpretation
8 – 12Normal AG
> 12High AG metabolic acidosis
< 8Low AG (rare)

Example

Sodium (Na⁺) = 140 mmol/L
Chloride (Cl⁻) = 104 mmol/L
Bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻) = 24 mmol/L

Calculation:
AG = 140 - (104 + 24) = 12
Interpretation: Normal Anion Gap

Clinical Notes

High AG metabolic acidosis can be remembered with MUDPILES: Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Paraldehyde/Phenformin, Iron/Isoniazid, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene glycol, Salicylates.

Low AG is rare, often due to hypoalbuminemia or lab error.
