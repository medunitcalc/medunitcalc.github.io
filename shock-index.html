Shock Index

Shock Index (SI) is a simple clinical indicator used to assess a patient’s hemodynamic status by comparing heart rate to systolic blood pressure.

Shock Index Formula

Shock Index = Heart Rate (bpm) ÷ Systolic Blood Pressure (mmHg)

Example

If heart rate is 100 bpm and systolic blood pressure is 120 mmHg:

Shock Index = 0.83

Normal Values

  • Normal: 0.5 – 0.7
  • Elevated: 0.7 – 0.9
  • High Risk: > 0.9

Clinical Importance

Shock Index is commonly used in emergency medicine and trauma education to identify early signs of circulatory failure.

Limitations

Shock Index must always be interpreted in the clinical context and should not be used alone for diagnosis.
