PaO₂ / FiO₂ Ratio (P/F Ratio)

The PaO₂ / FiO₂ ratio, or P/F ratio, is a key parameter to assess oxygenation efficiency in the lungs.

Formula

P/F Ratio = PaO₂ (mmHg) ÷ FiO₂ (Percentage)

Example

PaO₂ = 80 mmHg
FiO₂ = 40%

P/F Ratio = 200

Interpretation

P/F Ratio Clinical Meaning
> 300 Normal oxygenation
200 – 300 Mild hypoxemia
100 – 200 Moderate hypoxemia
< 100 Severe hypoxemia

Common Mistakes

  • Using FiO₂ as a percentage instead of a fraction
  • Forgetting units for PaO₂ (mmHg)
  • Interpreting P/F ratio without clinical context
