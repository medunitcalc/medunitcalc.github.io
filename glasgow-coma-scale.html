Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)

The Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) is a tool used to assess the level of consciousness after brain injury by evaluating eye, verbal, and motor responses.

How to Calculate GCS

GCS = Eye (E) + Verbal (V) + Motor (M)

Total score ranges from 3 (deep coma) to 15 (fully awake)

Interpretation

GCS ScoreInterpretation
13 – 15Mild brain injury
9 – 12Moderate brain injury
3 – 8Severe brain injury (coma)

Example

A patient has the following responses:
- Eye response: To speech → 3
- Verbal response: Confused → 4
- Motor response: Withdraws from pain → 4

Calculation:
GCS = 3 + 4 + 4 = 11
Interpretation: Moderate brain injury
